The Problem
Knowledge is scattered. Answers are inconsistent.
Horizon has grown 18 months fast — but its internal knowledge infrastructure hasn't kept pace.
350
Employees across 5 teams
5
Disconnected info sources
4
Known policy conflicts
18mo
Rapid growth period
  • Policy information is spread across documents, Slack messages, and manager decisions — no single source of truth
  • Employees receive inconsistent answers depending on who they ask — same question, different answers
  • Managers and HR spend disproportionate time on repetitive, low-value questions that should be self-service
  • Informal Slack guidance conflicts with formal policy in at least 4 documented cases — a compliance risk
  • Problem compounds with scale — every new hire adds to the question volume
Proposed Solution
An internal AI assistant that knows the policy — and when not to answer.
RAG-powered, Slack-native, source-cited, escalation-aware.
What it does
  • Answers policy questions in Slack — where employees already are
  • Searches Horizon's own approved documents — not the internet
  • Returns structured, source-cited answers in under 10 seconds
  • Detects policy conflicts and flags them rather than choosing a side
What it never does
  • Approve leave, reimbursements, or equipment requests
  • Guess when confidence is low — it escalates instead
  • Handle confidential employee data or client information
  • Answer questions outside the 4 scoped domains
Covers 4 domains in Version 1:   📋 Leave & Time-Off  |  💻 Equipment & Remote Work  |  💰 Expense Reimbursement  |  🤖 AI Tool Usage & Communication
System Design
How it works — end to end.
A retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipeline with guardrails and escalation routing.

via Slack Bot
🔍 Query cleaned & classified
📚 Knowledge base searched
Policy Notes 1–5
🧠 LLM generates answer
⚖️ Confidence check
✅ Answer delivered
⚠️ Escalate to human
💬
User Interface
Slack Bot (Bolt SDK). Zero new tool adoption — employees are already in Slack.
📚
Knowledge Base
5 approved policy notes, chunked and indexed in a vector database (Chroma/Pinecone).
🔍
Retrieval Layer
Semantic similarity search using text embeddings. Returns top-3 relevant policy chunks.
🧠
LLM Layer
Claude generates structured answers grounded in retrieved context only. No hallucination.
🛡️
Guardrails
Input filter blocks confidential data. Output check ensures answers are grounded in policy.
📊
Logging & Feedback
Every query, answer, and thumbs up/down logged. Used for weekly quality review.

Design Decisions
Four decisions that shaped the system.
Each decision has a reason and a trade-off the client should understand.
01
Source Authority
Formal policy notes over Slack messages
4 conflicts were found between informal Slack guidance and formal policy. The assistant always follows the formal note and flags the conflict. This ensures consistency and audit-readiness.

Trade-off: May contradict guidance employees have already received informally.
02
Approval Scope
The assistant informs — it never decides
"You are eligible to request this" is not the same as "your request is approved." Every response includes an explicit next step requiring human action. This prevents employees from bypassing the approval process.

Trade-off: Adds a step — employees still need to act after receiving an answer.
03
Confidence Threshold
Escalate rather than guess
Version 1 is built cautiously. Low-confidence answers escalate to a human instead of being delivered. A wrong confident answer causes more harm than a cautious escalation.

Trade-off: Some questions that could be automated will still go to HR in Version 1.
04
Interface Choice
Slack — zero new tool adoption
All 350 employees already use Slack. Building a separate web app would add onboarding friction. A Slack bot deploys faster and gets adopted immediately.

Trade-off: Slack bot UI has formatting constraints. Rich tables render differently per client.
Risk Management
Five risks — each with a concrete mitigation.
No AI system is risk-free. Honest acknowledgement and built-in mitigations are the right approach.
Risk Level Mitigation
Hallucination
LLM makes up a confident but unsupported answer		 HIGH LLM is constrained to only use retrieved policy content. Claims not in the retrieved text trigger automatic confidence downgrade before delivery.
Stale Policy Documents
Policy changes but knowledge base is not updated		 HIGH Each policy chunk is tagged with a version date and source owner. Monthly review process confirms currency. Outdated chunks are flagged for re-ingestion.
Policy Conflicts
4 known Slack vs. formal policy contradictions		 HIGH Conflict detection runs before answer generation. Conflicted responses are forced to Low confidence and always escalated — never delivered as a definitive answer.
Confidential Data Ingested
Employee pastes client data into a query		 MED Input pre-processing scans for sensitive data patterns before the query reaches the LLM. Flagged queries are blocked and the employee is prompted to rephrase.
Escalation Overload
Too many escalations defeat the purpose		 MED Escalation rate monitored weekly. If it exceeds 40%, retrieval quality and knowledge base coverage are reviewed. Target: ≥70% of questions answered without escalation.

Dependencies
What we need from Horizon before building starts.
Four organisational actions — the engineering team cannot proceed without them.
📄
Policy Document Sign-Off
Written confirmation from HR and Finance that all five policy notes are current and authoritative. Any outdated content must be updated before ingestion into the knowledge base.
⚠️
Resolution of 4 Policy Conflicts
Four conflicts were identified between informal Slack guidance and formal policy notes (food expenses, AI tool use, remote equipment eligibility, blocker escalation). The official position must be documented before the assistant can handle these questions correctly.
⚙️
IT Slack Bot Permission
IT must confirm that a Slack bot can be deployed to the company workspace and that all 350 employees have active accounts accessible to the bot.
👤
Designated Policy Owners
One named contact per domain who will receive escalated questions and confirm policy currency during monthly reviews: HR (Leave, Equipment), Finance (Reimbursements), IT (AI Tool Usage).
Critical path: The policy conflict resolution is not a technical item — it is an organisational one. The engineering team can build around it, but the assistant cannot correctly handle the four conflict scenarios until Horizon documents its official position. This should be the first action taken.
